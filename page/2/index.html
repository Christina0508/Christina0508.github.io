Tax revenue shows signs of recovery
By ZHOU LANXU | CHINA DAILY | Updated: 2020-07-18 07:58
A bank teller counts RMB banknotes at a bank branch in Hai’an of East China’s Jiangsu province, on June 24, 2020. [Photo/Sipa]
China’s fiscal revenue, which reported its first growth this year in June, returned to expansionary territory, enabling stronger fiscal support for the economy over the second half, experts said on Friday.
The government’s general budgeted revenue grew by 3.2 percent year-on-year last month, marking the first expansion this year, thanks to the recovery in business activity and imports, the Ministry of Finance said on Friday.
Fiscal revenue has been in a rally since the second quarter of the year as the economy staged comeback growth from the shutdowns brought by the COVID-19 outbreak, said Liu Jinyun, an official with the ministry.
Over the first half, fiscal revenue contracted by 10.8 percent year-on-year to 9.62 trillion yuan ($1.37 trillion), narrowing from 14.3 percent for the first quarter, according to Liu.
The year-on-year slide in the value-added tax revenue, the biggest contributor to the country’s national coffer, narrowed by 19.1 percent for the January-June period, versus 23.6 percent for the first three months as production activities in the industrial sector continued to recover, Liu said.
“The positive growth in fiscal revenue is a reflection of the nation’s steady economic recovery as well as the stable operation of market entities,” said Yang Weiyong, an associate professor of economics at the University of International Business and Economics in Beijing.
With the economy continuing to recover in the second half, fiscal revenue is expected to further rise and increase funds available for the government to support the economy, Yang said.
The country reported a 1.6 percent year-on-year contraction in GDP for the first half of the year on Thursday, recovering from the 6.8 percent plunge for the first quarter, while analysts expect China’s economic growth to come in at about 2 to 3 percent for the whole year.
Luo Zhiheng, former chief macroeconomic researcher at the Evergrande Research Institute, said the proactive fiscal policy has played a key role in facilitating the economic recovery, with targeted tax and fee cuts and relatively strong fiscal expenditure focused on combating the contagion, ensuring people’s livelihood and funding major construction projects.
The country’s fiscal spending in the first six months fell by 5.8 percent from a year earlier to 11.64 trillion yuan, slower than the drop in revenue, according to the ministry.Looking into the second half, Luo said the country is expected to accelerate fiscal spending and shore up domestic demand, with the recovery in fiscal revenue and money raised by government
bonds providing more funds.
China has decided to issue 1 trillion yuan in special treasuries for COVID-19 control efforts this year. As of Thursday, a total of 720 billion yuan of the bonds had been issued, with the rest to be issued by the end of this month, the ministry said.
The country has also stepped up the issuance of the projected 3.75 trillion yuan worth of special local government bonds, which are mainly to fund infrastructure investment. A total of 2.24 trillion yuan worth of the bonds were issued as of Tuesday, with 85 percent of the money raised already be spent, the ministry said.
“The Chinese economy still faces the risk that the global spread of COVID-19 may hit external demand, threatening employment and the survival of small and medium-sized enterprises.
Extending supports to these areas will be the focus of fiscal policy in the coming months,” Luo said.
Nonetheless, analysts said it is unlikely that China will elevate the quota of special treasuries, given the need to prevent excessive stimulus from causing risk.
“To prevent the distortion in resource allocation, it is more advisable for China to further the tax and fee cut measures than issue more bonds to fund government-led investment,” Yang said.

How reducing tax revenue to help recover
Because of the rise of the novel coronavirus in the world, the recovery is the most important aim of China, in order to recover, linking to the concept tax which means that a compulsory contribution to state revenue, levied by the government on workers’ income and business profits, or added to the cost of some goods, services, and transactions.I want to discuss whether it’s helpful for recovery and why it offers a helping hand to recover.
From the news, we can get that “Over the first half, fiscal revenue contracted by 10.8 percent year-on-year to 9.62 trillion yuan ($1.37 trillion), narrowing from 14.3 percent for the first quarter.”In this way, we can know that the fiscal revenue of the first quarter of 2020 is more than that of the revenue of the second quarter. This may because the indirect tax and direct tax all decreases. Firstly, government gives back some tax to people that are directly charged from their salaries. Secondly, government may also reduce the prices of products especially the prices of
necessities. In this way, people will be more affordable for these products. As a result, people can balance their lives during the novel coronavirus.

From the news, we can get that “China has decided to issue 1 trillion yuan in special treasuries for COVID-19 control efforts this year. As of Thursday, a total of 720 billion yuan of the bonds had been issued, with the rest to be issued by the end of this month.” In order to support the further recovery, the government has issued 1 trillion yuan in special treasuries to help state-owned business. As a result, the prices of products will decrease, people will be more affordable for them. The economic growth can grow. However, issuing money cannot help recovering very well, because of the virus, although people can be more affordable for products, they may have no confidence for the future, and they will save the money instead of spending money. As a result, money of government has wasted and has no effects. In this way, there is little help for recovery. In addition, issuing the money may lead the distortion in resource allocation because it’s difficult for the government to estimate how much money to give which sector, so it’s difficult to spend money effectively.

If government reduces the tax revenue, not only the prices of the products reduce, but also people’s salaries increase (both direct tax and indirect tax). In this way, people will have money to afford products. Although we may face the same problem, that’s people with no confidence about future, government may face a different situation. In conclusion, issuing the money is easy to get to lead the distortion in resource allocation, so reduce tax revenue may be is a more reasonable method to help recovery. Water quenches thirst for growth
By Fang Aiqing in Beijing and Hu Dongmei in Pengyang, Ningxia | China Daily | Updated: 2020-06-13 09:14
Over the past decade, Ningxia has been prioritizing the resolution to provide rural areas with safe drinking water. Reliable supply transforms once barren area into a hive of productivity and profit.

With the help of automation and smart technology, residents of one of China’s driest areas are getting affordable clean water instead of yearning for rainfall.

It took people living in the central and south of Ningxia Hui autonomous region nearly 40 years to complete this arduous journey.

“In the past, we had to pour the water into the jar and let it settle before drinking it,” says Li Xiaoqin, a villager at Chengyang village of Pengyang county in the southeast of the autonomous region.
Zhang Shixiang, Party secretary of the village, says they used to carry water two kilometers to home, and it took two hours just to get the water.

“We didn’t have drinking water, let alone water to shower,” Li says, adding that they now have tap water not only to drink and shower, but also to clean the house, water the flowers, feed livestock, and importantly, flush the toilet.
Experts from the Ministry of Water Resources visit Pengyang county in the southeast of the autonomous region.

Today, the nearly 200,000 people living in the countryside of Pengyang, can, through phone access, check water quality and pay fees. Crucially, water charges are the same as those for townsfolk.

The southern mountainous area of the autonomous region, including where the county is located, is often referred to as Xihaigu.

Forty years ago, it was a dry and rainless place where barren hills stretched on and on and ravines and gullies crisscrossed. Groundwater was scarce.

Water resource per capita was less than one-sixth of the average amount nationwide, according to Jiang Qi, a member of the National Committee of the Chinese People’s Political Consultative Conference and director of the institute for desertification control at the Ningxia Academy of Agricultural and Forestry Sciences.

Scarcity of water had once been a constraint for the region’s high-quality development, he says.
A regional government document on poverty alleviation released in September 2016 shows that rain-fed farming accounted for 90 percent of total farmlands in underdeveloped areas of the autonomous region, and 80 percent of the cultivated land yielded not much and unsteadily.

Yet, annual rainfall of the county ranges from 350 to 550 millimeters, around one-third of the amount of evaporation, and rainfall from July through September takes up more than 60 percent of the whole year, making the situation in other seasons even worse, according to a government introduction of Pengyang’s natural geographic features.

However, with a stable water source now, the locals are actively planting red plum apricots (a local specialty), pepper, edible mushrooms and Chinese medical herbs, and also raising chickens, bees, cattle and sheep.

Niu Detan, general manager of the county’s water supply management company, says some locals who used to work outside have come back to run breeding businesses, or operate greenhouses and plant orchards.

People’s income is growing every year, Niu says.
All this is thanks to a water transfer project that makes it possible for more than one million people of the Xihaigu area to access safe drinking water since 2016.

The project transfers water from the Jinghe River by the east foot of the Liupan Mountain in southern Ningxia. The river is a tributary of the Weihe River, which, itself, is a tributary of the Yellow River.

Based on the project, the county government of Pengyang invested further to integrate updated internet technology specially to improve the quality and stability of water supply in rural areas.

Zhang admitted that facilities didn’t work well at first and water quality was not satisfying.
The county later transformed the facilities in the reservoir, the pump station and the pipe network, and equipped every household with smart water meters, which enables real-time video monitoring of the whole water supply process, as well as remote control and management on computer and phone.

The water management staff can monitor the PH value and turbidity of the water online. Once there’s a breakdown, the system would automatically sound the alarm and distribute maintenance tasks.

According to Niu, if there were a communication failure at the pump station, the system inside the station would be able to supply water steadily for at least a week.

The intelligent facilities have solved the problem of high cost and management difficulty caused by rural households being numerous but scattered, Jiang says.

Therefore, water fees are lowered in these underdeveloped villages where pipelines have to go through mountains and ditches.

As Ningxia has already built a unified online platform before to manage water conservancy information, and facilities to provide internet access for rural areas, the county doesn’t have to invest much on internet infrastructure for the transformation such as server and the machine room, nor the data operation and security issues, according to Niu.
Over the past decade, Ningxia has been prioritizing the resolution to provide rural areas with safe drinking water. Tap water penetration has risen from 26 percent at the end of 2006 to 90 percent by 2019.

“We have historically solved the problem of water shortage and covered the barren hills with green,” says Ma Hancheng, vice-chairman of the autonomous region and a deputy to the National People’s Congress at a recent interview with chinanews.com.
Ningxia is now seeking to promote the intelligent water supply facilities of Pengyang to the whole region and is determined to provide replicable and referential experience for the country, where freshwater resource is much limited and distributes unevenly.
The region is calling for more financial and policy support from the country, according to a recent proposal from the region’s water conservancy department.

代言中的奥秘

title: 代言中的奥秘
date: 2020-06-14 11:10:30
tags: 时尚篇2
—代言中的奥秘
在上一篇中，提到了杂志的销售情况与代言息息相关。当一本杂志在线预售时，就会有粉丝购买，他们的购买意图是为自己的偶像获得更好的商业资源。

通过线上预售的方式，杂志方可以得到电子的销售数据，而这些数据就是提供给各大品牌方的资料，微博粉丝数量都不能说明什么，只有数据可以说明商业价值，评价一个明星是否是实红就是他的粉丝会不会为他买单，粉丝和流量是评价一个明星的层次的中重要依据（一线，二线等）而这与商业价值中的测层次确实大不相同的，在商业价值有会为明星评价一个明星新的依据。

以陈伟霆为例，他是一个典型的商业价值非常高的艺人，冰淇淋-中街1946在2019年一年的销售额大约为一亿元，已经是冷饮中的爆款，在2020年5月10日官宣陈伟霆作为代言人的当天的销售额为两千万，可见陈伟霆的带货能力之强，而我们可以看到陈伟霆的微博粉丝数量并不是特别的多，与流量明星相比相差很多，在各大数据榜单上也并没有很靠前的位置，但是他的商业价值非常的高，例如他曾经在同一时期代言两款手机，oppo和国美plus，代言香飘飘的饮品和卫岗酸奶和百威啤酒。一般来说品牌方会避免请对手品牌的代言人做代言，同样的代言人就失去了竞争力，而陈伟霆同时代言两款手机，还代言多款饮品，说明了品牌方评估了陈伟霆的商业价值，认为他在为竞争对手代言的同时可以为他们的产品带来很好的收益经过和其他明星的对比。显而易见，更好的，更高端的品牌会找到更具有商业价值更红的明星来做代言。

那为什么明星的商业价值会有这么大的差异，为什么很多大流量的商业价值很低，而且接到的代言都是低档次的日常用品饮品食品呢？这与粉丝的受众群体十分相关。市场上的大流量大部分都有个特点，低龄，这与正是他们能够成为流量的原因，低龄的人群更容易被吸引成为粉丝，他们的粉丝大多数人都是学生，学生经济不独立，家长很难给他们足够的钱来满足为偶像消费的需求，让他们只能成为不消费的粉丝，也就是死粉，可以认定为没有购买力的，但他们又是那些流量明星的流量来源。这些低龄的粉丝只能够购买价格便宜的商品，所以那些廉价的生活用品就成为了流量明星的代言。而像陈伟霆这样年龄的明星，粉丝的受众群体大都是经济独立的女性，他们可以支配自己的资金，他们需要在生活中购买一些奢侈用品，例如陈伟霆就是香奈儿的代言人。这就成为了他们商业的依据，也就铸造成了粉丝数和商业价值的巨大差异。

代言和杂志中的奥秘，我暂时分解这么多，更多资讯敬请期待。

经过对比后，我发现这几本封面人物不同的杂志，其实虽说都是同一本杂志，但是本质上却十分的不同。以费加罗为例，费加罗本身属于在杂志分类中的女刊，并非男刊，虽然经常请女明星作为封面人物，但是也会请男明星有作为正刊的封面人物，而我们之前所说的五月刊的正刊的封面为黄子韬，众所周知的大众流量明星。而高伟光封面的这一版是的Mode的特别刊，所谓Mode刊并非正刊，相当于我们常说的副刊，所以封面的人物会咖位会比正刊人物小但是又比二线杂志的封面人物的咖位要大。而特别刊并非简单的Mode刊，而是为了某个特定的主题·，或是某个特定的人物而出版的。而体现在杂志上的不同为，普通的Mode刊只有一版的封面，而特别刊有a，b两版的封面,而且限量发售，所以显而易见，特别刊的人物咖位比普通Mode刊要大，但是却小于正刊人物。此外，费加罗本身为一本女刊，但是在每年的四月和十月都会出一本男刊，此男刊为正刊，封面上会有Hommes的字样，体现为男刊，这本与前面说到的女刊的正刊同等分量，人物咖位相当。

杂志将不同的刊号分的十分精细，是因为，在复杂的娱乐圈中，所谓的明星，其中有非常重要的一项为商业价值，那怎么样才能够最合理的评估商业价值，那么就要用到杂志的帮助了，根据明星的粉丝数量和活跃程度，以及电视剧电影的作品的热度，也就是本人实力和流量，杂志方会根据这些邀请明星作为杂志封面，很显而易见，一线杂志邀请到的为一线明星，而二线杂志则为二线明星，但这些都是杂志方根据上述的各种标准得到的评估，那么此时会出现所为的“实红”和“水红”。刚刚说到，这些杂志是限量发售的，但是其实方法为预售，预售就是在杂志就还没有印刷出来之前先以网络购买的方式来销售。以这样的方法可以方便评估实际印刷的本数，防止印刷出来后卖不掉，因为经常有粉丝数量多，但是销售量一直上不去的名星，这就是粉丝不愿意为他们买单的结果，也就是上面提到的名词“水红”。这就是杂志为了防止这种情况发生的措施。而且在饭圈中有一个词叫做“秒切”，这个词的意思就是在几秒钟的时间之内将预售的数量售罄，因为这些将作为数据。

— ——自然语言处理
字母，文字，数字都是编码单位。一种语言就是一种编码的方式，语法就是解编码的算法。语言的数学本质就是通过编码来表达信息。虽说动物也能传递信息，但是直邮人类利用语言传递信息是人类的本质。随着计算机的出现，机器也可以用人类的方式处理和传递语言。智能就是人和机器交流，当人能够和机器交流时，说明这个机器就是智能了。

许多的科学家认为机器能够翻译或者识别等人类做的事，首先是要了解人类语言，而这一点是错误的，是因为计算机能够解释和做人类做的事，全都是因为数学，数学中的统计。而我们计算机语言的核心就是能够理解语法规则，就是对自然语言的处理。分析句子的文法被科学家用做规则来书写。所以高级程序语言的规则和自然语言的规则看上去很相似。然后人们分析更复杂的句子就要用从一维变成二维了，我们无法将图画出来，所以单纯对语言分析器已经无法分析这么复杂的语句了。但是即使写出所有自然语言对合集也无法用计算机来表示所有。即使是很规则的方法也不能分析一些真实语句。

自然界中很多的多义词很难用规则来描述，这就是计算机对难处，很多的意思依赖于上下文，或者常识。于是便从规则到了统计，用统计的办法使计算机的识别率大大提高。基于同统计的方法只能浅层次的理解自然语言，而无法从深层次研究。由于计算机能力的提高和数据量的增加，渐渐的分析复杂句子变成了可能，大家发现运用统计得到的句法比语言学家总结的更加的有道理。所以用数学处理自然语言。而科学家的新老交替才带来了发展。所以数学意模型和通讯是相通的，科学家们花了几十年来理解期中的联系。

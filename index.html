Things important for me

Posted on 2022-03-22

Confidence has always been an essential part for me. It came from my early experience in learning swimming. At the beginning, I could not stay in water without a ring and was afraid of drawning in water. The teacher taught me to overcome the fear step by step and always reminded me to believe in myself. The great sense of accomplishment from the experience managed to motivate me with a confident attitude.
In school’s one important exam, I felt very nervous and found it really difficult, which led to a far worse exam result than usual. Exposed in the mood of despair, I asked myself to cheer up. I knew that everyone would not question my ability and knowledge from this single failure, as I was always capable to overcome these challenges like how I learned to swim. I soon recovered from the negative emotion and began to review the reasons for the terrible exam; this failure became an important learning opportunity as it helped me to navigate the overlooked issues in my reviews. I am more confident to combat further challenges afterwards.
Extending from my own experience, I have always been trying to preach my confident attitudes to the others. I tell my friends that I trust them and they should trust themselves whenever they face failures like I did. In group assignments specifically, my attitude allowed the group to critically reflect on any issue together to find a solution, believing that we can overcome whatever difficult.

Pandemic influences

Posted on 2021-08-14

During the pandemic, museums and libraries were closed, so they did not need volunteers to do off-line services, making it hard for me to connect with my volunteer community.
In order to spread the museum cultures, I suggested to call volunteers to write articles for the museum’s Wechat official account as alternative offline participation. I wrote one poem to relate the museum and plum blossom mountain. In addition, we recorded videos at home to encourage people to spread out positive energy on behalf of the volunteer program. I also read books and made podcasts on the library’s social media to preach some overlooked facts and knowledge.
I noticed that, despite the working forms of volunteers have changed a lot, the pandemic haven’t interrupted people’s enthusiasm to continue being helpful. With this key mindset, nothing challenging, including the Pandemic, could not stop idea and thought spreads. I was honored to be together with the group of volunteers to truly devote my contribution in helping out the museum’s promotions and preaching new knowledge to various groups of people. The pandemic seemed to have forced us to be apart, but our practices binded the community with deeper connections.
